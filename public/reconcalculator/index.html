🧤 Prepare Your Workspace

  • Wash hands thoroughly and put on sterile gloves and eye protection
  • Clean your work surface and lay out all necessary supplies
  • Gather: peptide vial, bacteriostatic water, sterile syringe, and alcohol swabs

🌡️ Warm to Room Temperature

  • Remove peptide and bacteriostatic water from the refrigerator
  • Allow both vials to reach room temperature before proceeding
  • Cold solutions can interfere with proper dissolution

🧪 Reconstitution Steps

  • Remove the center cap from the peptide vial
  • Wipe the rubber stopper with an alcohol swab — allow to fully dry
  • Repeat the same cleaning process for the bacteriostatic water vial
  • Draw the required amount of BAC water into a sterile syringe
  • Tilt the peptide vial at a 45° angle
  • Inject the BAC water slowly down the inside wall of the vial — do not squirt directly onto the powder
  • Gently swirl to mix — do not shake vigorously
  • The solution should be clear with no visible particles before use

🧊 Storage Guidelines

  • Store reconstituted peptide in the refrigerator at 2–8°C (36–46°F)
  • Use reconstituted solution within 3–4 weeks when refrigerated
  • For longer-term storage, freeze at −20°C — stable for 3–4 months
  • Avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles, as this can degrade the peptide
  • Store lyophilized (unreconstituted) peptide at −20°C or below for long-term stability
